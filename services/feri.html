Democratic Republic of the Congo FERI – Freight Certificate

"Fiche Electronique des Renseignements a L’ Importation," which is a French acronym. It is a document that must accompany all sea shipments destined for the Democratic Republic of Congo. The appointed party responsible for the shipment must ensure that the applicable FERI Certification has been issued,or they may face severe penalties. The Democratic Republic of Congo Customs Officials mandated in 2011 that the CTN/BSC is compulsory for ports including Matadi, Boma, Banana, and others.

How To Acquire The Democratic Republic of Congo FERI Certificate!

To acquire the democratic republic of Congo fERI certificate you need to submit the following documents:

Transport Document – Draft Bill of Lading, Air-Waybill, or CMR copy can be used for the draft.

Freight Invoice – (only necessary if the freight value is NOT indicated on commercial invoice)

Commercial Invoice – Incoterm, reference, and date must be mentioned.

FXI Number – The importer has to provide it (not mandatory)

Democratic Republic of Congo Feri Regulations

Shippers or forwarder agents have to submit the FERI certificate 5 days before the vessel arrives at the territorial waters of the Democratic Republic of Congo. If FERI is absent, the FERI issuance will be paid at the arrival, plus a fine of 20% on the freight value. (409/021 – 23rd March 1992)

Important Notice

Export companies are advised to obtain approval for their FERI certificates at least five days prior to the arrival of the ship in the territorial waters of the Democratic Republic of the Congo. The manifest is used to calculate this period, and it's crucial to prevent any delays. To ensure timely approval, it's recommended that the application be submitted immediately after the ship's departure. The FERI number can only be viewed on certificates that have been paid and approved. To comply with certain letters of credit, it's advisable to include all FERI numbers on the bill of lading.
